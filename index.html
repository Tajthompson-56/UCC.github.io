Admissions

University of the Commonwealth Caribbean

Programme Overview

The Bachelor of Science in Information Technology is a four-year programme worth 124 credits. It is available via Blended and 100% Online delivery through UCC Global. The next start date is May (Summer) 2026.

The programme produces graduates with technical, business, and interpersonal skills to make meaningful contributions to the IT industry nationally, regionally, and globally.

Duration

UCC Bachelor of Science degree programmes are four-year programmes. This may be reduced by up to two years if a student gains Advanced Standing with an appropriate Associate of Science degree or equivalent qualification.

Entry Requirements — Jamaican Students

  • Minimum of five (5) subjects at GCE or CSEC level including English Language and Mathematics at grades A, B, C or 1, 2, 3 respectively. A CSEC Grade 3 must have been obtained since 1998.
  • Candidates with four (4) CXCs may apply pending outstanding subjects or may opt to complete UCC replacement courses: Core Mathematics, English for Academic Purposes, and Fundamentals of Accounting.
  • Applicants may apply under the Mature Entry option — must be at least 25 years old with a minimum of five (5) years of work experience. Academic qualifications, résumé, job letter, and three professional references are required.

Entry Requirements — International Students

  • Application fee (non-refundable) of US$100
  • Completed Application for Admission
  • High school diploma, GED, or Certificate of Equivalency/Proficiency with a minimum GPA of 2.5
  • Passes in 5 GCE O Level subjects at A, B, or C including Mathematics and English, or International Baccalaureate (IB) or equivalent qualification
  • One electronic passport size photo
  • Copy of passport information page
  • Financial documentation demonstrating ability to support yourself during your stay in Jamaica (with completed UCC Affidavit of Support Form)
  • Official transcripts of all secondary school and university coursework (translated in English), evaluated by one of the following:
    • www.acei1.com
    • www.ierf.org
    • www.wes.org

Honours Programme

Students with eight (8) CXC/GCE subjects including Mathematics and English Language at Grade 1 may qualify for a 25% tuition waiver during the first year.
